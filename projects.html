FIRST Robotics Competition

The FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is an annual international event where high school students design, build, and program robots to compete in a specific game or challenge. Teams have six weeks to create their robots before competing in regional events. The competition promotes STEM skills, teamwork, and gracious professionalism.

I spent 3 Years in FRC(FIRST Robotics Competition):
  • First year: I was in independed team called Golder Horn 8159. I attend severel reginols with this team and one of them was in San Francisco. We won the Engineering award, by that we got the right to attend World Finals in Houston.
  • Second Year: I decided to open my own team in my high school called Callister 9024. I was the youth mentor of the team. We won severel awards but we were unable to atten World Finals.
  • Third Year: I became the mentor of my team (Callister) and also we decided to open another team in the same high schools. by that I became mentor of 2 teams. 2nd team earn right to attend World Finals by winning the Robotics Competition.

VEX Robotics Competition

The VEX Robotics Competition is where students build and program robots to complete tasks on a playing field. It's organized by the Robotics Education & Competition Foundation and promotes teamwork and STEM skills.

My VEX adventure was short lived. Because we only attend 1 competition, shut down the team afterwards.
